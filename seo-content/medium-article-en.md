Spain's DGT theory exam has a 40-50% failure rate on the first attempt. It's not because it's impossible — it's because most people don't know how to prepare properly.

I'll show you the system that actually works, including the best apps available in English.

## What the exam actually looks like

- **30 multiple-choice questions** (3 answer options each)
- **Maximum 3 mistakes allowed** — the 4th mistake = fail
- **30 minutes** total
- Questions with **deliberate traps** — DGT uses words like "always", "never", "only" to trick you

The exam tests **understanding**, not memorization. That's why people who just read the manual without practicing fail.

## Why most people fail

1. They memorize answers without understanding the rules
2. They do too few practice tests (under 50)
3. They never practice under real exam conditions (time pressure, 30 questions in one go)
4. They don't analyze their mistakes

## The system that works: 3 phases

### Phase 1: Smart theory (days 1-3)
Don't read the full manual. Focus on:
- Traffic signs (about 30% of the exam)
- Right-of-way at intersections
- Speed limits by road type
- Alcohol & drugs rules (very frequent on the exam)
- Overtaking rules (many traps here)

### Phase 2: Massive test practice (days 4-14)
Minimum 100 tests. Ideally 200+.

The key: **analyze every wrong answer** before moving on. Don't just rack up numbers — understand why you got it wrong.

### Phase 3: Full simulations (days 15-20)
Practice under real exam conditions: 30 questions, 30 minutes, no distractions.

When you're consistently getting 0-1 mistakes, you're ready.

## The trick questions DGT loves (memorize these)

**Right-of-way at intersections:**
DGT loves edge cases. Basic rule: right > left, but there are exceptions (roundabouts, signs, trams). Learn them all.

**Distances (these appear constantly):**
- Minimum 50m signal distance before overtaking
- 5m from pedestrian crossings for parking
- 3m from tram lines

**Alcohol limits:**
- General drivers: 0.5 g/l blood / 0.25 mg/l breath
- Professional drivers & new drivers: 0.3 g/l / 0.15 mg/l

**Signs:**
DGT confuses similar-looking signs. Learn not just the meaning but the visual differences.

## Exam day strategy

1. Read all 30 questions before answering any
2. Answer the ones you're sure about first
3. For doubts: eliminate obviously wrong answers first
4. Don't change an answer without a solid reason (first instinct is usually right)
5. If you see "always" or "never" — be suspicious. DGT rarely uses absolutes correctly as right answers

## As an expat: practical tips

- **Book your exam** early — appointments fill up weeks in advance at some traffic departments
- You need a **NIE** and valid residence to take the exam
- The exam is available in Spanish only at DGT centers, but you can study in English with Skilyapp
- Once you pass theory, you have 2 years to pass the practical (driving) test
